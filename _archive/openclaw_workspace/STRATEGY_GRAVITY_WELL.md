The Gravity Well Protocol - Trading the Invisible Accumulation

Introduction
While traditional trend-following strategies (like The Tao of Trading) wait for the train to leave the station ("surfing the wave"), The Gravity Well Protocol is designed to find the train while it is still boarding. This strategy targets "Order Book Anomalies"—specific moments where heavy volume hits a stock, yet the price refuses to move. This phenomenon creates a "Gravity Well," indicating that a massive buyer (an iceberg order) is absorbing all available supply. We do not look for "beautiful" charts; we look for "coiled" energy in ugly or boring charts just before they explode.

I. The Core Philosophy: Volume Efficiency
The market lies with price, but it tells the truth with volume. The central thesis of the Gravity Squeeze is Volume Efficiency.

A. The Gravity Score
In a normal market, high volume creates high volatility (a large candle). Normal: 1M volume -> 10% move. The Anomaly: 1M volume -> 1% move. When massive volume results in almost zero price movement, it means every single share being sold is being instantly absorbed by a hidden buyer at a fixed price. This is the Gravity Well.
Formula: Relative Volume / Price Range %
Meaning: If a stock has 3x normal volume (RVOL 3.0) but only moves 1%, the Anomaly Score is 3.0. This is a "glitch" in the matrix. It implies price manipulation or heavy accumulation.

B. The Anti-Trend Mindset
Tao/Trend Traders: Buy after the breakout, when the moving averages are stacked and the chart looks "clean."
Gravity Traders: Buy inside the mess, when the chart looks "boring," "choppy," or "dead." We buy the potential energy (the squeeze), not the kinetic energy (the trend).

II. Anatomy of the Setup
A valid Gravity Squeeze requires three specific components visible in the scan data:
1. The Gravity Anomaly (The "Whale Footprint")
We look for a Gravity Anomaly Score > 1.1. This metric compares Relative Volume (RVOL) to the Price Range.
Formula: Relative Volume / Price Range %
Meaning: If a stock has 3x normal volume (RVOL 3.0) but only moves 1%, the Anomaly Score is 3.0. This is a "glitch" in the matrix. It implies price manipulation or heavy accumulation.
2. The Squeeze (The "Coil")
Energy must be compressing. We measure this using the Squeeze Ratio.
Criteria: Daily ATR < (Weekly ATR / 2)
Visual: The daily candles are getting smaller and tighter compared to the previous weeks. The stock is "going to sleep" just before waking up.
ADX: We look for ADX < 30. We specifically want absence of a trend.
3. The Spark (The Visual Trigger)
Once the Gravity Well is identified, we look for a specific candle shape on the daily chart:
🔨 The Hammer: Long lower wick with a small body. This shows sellers tried to flush the price, but the "Gravity" (buyer) absorbed it all and pushed it back up.
✨ The Doji: A tiny body with equal wicks. Total indecision and maximum compression.
🕳️ The Gravity Coil: A high volume day with an incredibly small range (e.g., < 2% range on > 1.5x volume).

III. Execution Rules
A. Asset Selection
This strategy is tuned for specific types of stocks:
Price: $0.50 - $5.00 (Micro-caps and Small-caps).
Market Cap: Under $2 Billion.
Personality: Stocks that have been beaten down or moving sideways for weeks. "The uglier the chart, the better the squeeze."

B. Entry Triggers
Do not buy blindly. Wait for the "Spark" confirmation.
The Wick Entry: If a Hammer appears, place a buy limit order near the top of the wick or a buy stop order just above the Hammer's high.
The Range Break: If a Gravity Coil (tight range) forms, buy when price breaks the previous day's high.
Volume Confirmation: Entry is only valid if RVOL (Relative Volume) is rising. We need the "heartbeat" to be active.

C. Stop Loss (The Invalidation Point)
Because we are buying before the trend, our risk is very tight.
Hard Stop: Just below the low of the "Spark" candle (Hammer or Doji).
Structural Stop: If the price closes below the "Gravity Well" zone (the tight consolidation range), the accumulation has failed. Get out immediately.

D. Time Stop
If the stock does not expand within 3-5 days of entry, the "spark" was a dud. Exit and free up capital. Gravity trades should work quickly.

E. Profit Targets (The Expansion)
We buy compression to sell expansion.
Target 1 (The Pop): +10% to +15%. Sell 1/2 position. Move stop to Breakeven.
Target 2 (The Runner): Trail your stop using the low of the previous day. Let the "Tao" trend followers buy your shares as the chart starts to look "good" to them.

IV. Risk Management & Psychology
The "Operating Expense" of Hunting
Unlike trend following, where win rates can be high, Gravity Trading is about Asymmetric Risk. You might be wrong often (the stock does nothing or breaks down).
Rule: Losses must be small (1-3% account risk).
Reward: When a Gravity Well explodes, it often moves 20-50% in a few days. One winner pays for 4-5 small losers ("scratches").
The Psychology of "Boring"
The Trap: You will feel the urge to sell because "nothing is happening."
The Discipline: Gravity Wells are boring right until they aren't. If the volume is there but price isn't moving, someone is building a position. Patience is your edge.
Warning: If Volume drops off and the stock drifts lower, the Gravity has dissipated. Exit.

[ ] Squeeze: Is the daily range compressing compared to last week? (Squeeze Ratio < 1.0)
